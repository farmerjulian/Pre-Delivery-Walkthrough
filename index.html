How your van charges

Your van keeps its battery topped up three different ways. Most of the time it all happens automatically — you just need to understand what each source does and when to lean on it.

🔗 Three ways in, one battery bank
Think of the system as three input lanes all feeding into your battery bank. The battery then powers everything else in the van.
☀️ Solar
Roof panels feed the Victron MPPT controller. Automatic, silent, runs all day in sunlight.
🚗 Alternator
While driving, the Orion DC-DC charger tops up your house battery automatically.
🔌 Shore power
Plug into a 30A campground pedestal or a standard home outlet. MultiPlus handles the rest.
☀️ What to expect from solar
Solar is great for maintaining your battery during light everyday use. It's not designed to keep up with big draws like AC, a cooktop, or a microwave — for those you'll want to plug in or drive.
! Winter solar output can drop 70–80% due to shorter days and lower sun angles. Plan to plug in more often in cold months.
🚗 Alternator charging while you drive
As soon as the engine is running, the Orion DC-DC charger starts routing alternator power to your house battery. You might notice a short ramp-up delay — that's normal. A couple hours of driving gives you meaningful charge recovery.
✓ Zero effort required — just drive and the battery takes care of itself.
🔌 Shore power
The fastest and most reliable way to charge. Plug into a 30A campground pedestal or use the included adapter for a standard home outlet. The MultiPlus automatically charges the battery and powers your 120V outlets simultaneously.
i For charging to work: Battery Disconnect must be ON and inverter set to ON or Charger Only mode.

Interior tour

Let's walk through the living space — drawers, cabinets, and the two things that surprise people most: push-to-lock slides and the electromagnet drawer system.

🗄️ Drawers & cabinets
Your build has its own layout, but most vans include a mix of standard pull drawers, overhead storage, and a few hidden compartments. Some drawers have soft-close slides, others have push-to-lock.
👆 Push-to-lock drawer slides
Some drawers — typically the hidden ones — use push-to-lock slides instead of handles. Push the drawer closed until you feel it click, and it locks in place. To open, push in again and it releases. No handle, no latch, just a push.
✓ Once you know the location, these hidden drawers disappear seamlessly into the cabinetry.
🧲 Electromagnet drawer locks
Some drawers are held closed by electromagnets that engage automatically when the van's ignition is on. This keeps things from sliding open while you're driving. When you turn the ignition off, the magnets release and the drawers open normally.
i There's a dedicated switch that breaks the magnet connection — so if you need to access those drawers while the engine is running, flip that switch and the magnets disengage.

Main controls

Your control panel is the command center of the van. Depending on your build you'll have a Webasto SmarTemp controller, a Victron inverter switch, a Cerbo GX touchscreen — or a combination.

🔥 Webasto heater controller
The Webasto SmarTemp 3.0 controls your air heater. Turn on and set your desired temperature — the heater does the rest. It pulls fuel directly from the van's own tank, so whether your vehicle runs on diesel or gas, the heater uses that same fuel. No separate tank to manage.
! When you turn it off, let it finish its cool-down cycle before cutting power. The fan needs to run for a few minutes to protect the internals.
🔀 Inverter — three modes
Your Victron inverter has three positions. This is one of the most important things to understand in the whole van:
ON Battery powers all 110V outlets and appliances. Use this when off-grid or not plugged in. Microwave, cooktop, AC, and water heater all work.
Charger Only Shore power runs your outlets and charges the battery. The battery itself is not used to power 110V loads. Use this when plugged in.
OFF No 110V outlets. Only 12V systems work — lights, fans, USB ports, the heater. Use for storage or to conserve battery.
✓ Simple rule: plugged in → Charger Only. Off-grid → ON. Storing the van → OFF.
📺 Cerbo GX touchscreen (if equipped)
Some builds have a Cerbo GX touchscreen instead of (or alongside) a physical inverter switch. It shows a live overview of your whole electrical system — battery SOC, solar input, shore power status, and inverter mode — all in one place. You can also switch inverter modes directly from the screen.
i Which control setup you have depends on your specific build. Your in-person walkthrough will cover exactly what's in your van.

Plumbing system

Your van has a complete fresh and grey water system. Here's how to fill it, use it, and find all the controls — which are split across two locations.

🪣 Filling the fresh water tank
The fill port is located inside the plumbing cabinet at the rear of the van — open the cabinet door and connect your hose there. Use a clean potable water hose only, as regular garden hoses can leach chemicals into your water supply.
✓ The fresh tank has an overflow — even if you overfill it, water drains outside the van rather than inside. No stress.
👁️ Checking your water level
There's a cutout in the plumbing cabinet door that lets you see directly into the tank area — no gauge, no glass. Just open the cabinet and look through the cutout to get a quick visual read on how much water you have.
🔩 Water filter & push-to-connect fittings
Your system includes an inline water filter — all water passes through it before reaching your fixtures. The fittings throughout are push-to-connect with anti-drip, meaning they're tool-free and won't drip when disconnected. Makes filter swaps and winterizing simple.
🔘 Plumbing switches — two locations
Water pump
Grey tank valves Inside the kitchen cabinet behind the trash door. Pump on/off and grey tank valve controls are both here.
Fresh tank dump valve Inside the rear plumbing cabinet — same door as the water fill port and level cutout.
! Always turn the pump OFF when leaving the van. Running it dry causes damage.
🚿 Using the fixtures
Turn the pump ON before using any water. Open a faucet and the pump pressurizes the lines. Turn it OFF when you're done. Hot and cold controls work as you'd expect — just make sure the pump is on first.
⏱️ Water heater — on a timer
The water heater switch turns on the heating element, but it runs on a timer so it won't stay on and drain your battery. Flip it on a few minutes before you need hot water — it'll time out automatically.
✓ No need to remember to turn it off — the timer handles that for you.

Electrical cabinet

This is where all the electrical components live — the brain of the system. You won't need to come in here often, but it's good to know what's inside.

👁️ What's inside
The electrical cabinet houses your Victron components — MultiPlus inverter/charger, MPPT solar charge controller, SmartShunt, and Orion DC-DC charger — along with your fuse block and WFCO breaker panel.
🛑 You mostly won't need to touch this
The system runs itself. The two situations where you might need to open this cabinet:
✓ Check or replace a fuse if something suddenly stops working
✓ Reset a tripped breaker on the WFCO panel
✓ If something isn't working, the breaker panel is the first place to look — a tripped breaker is the most common cause and the easiest fix.

Connect your phone

The last thing we do at every delivery — get VictronConnect on your phone so you can see exactly what your battery is doing, anytime.

📲 Step 1 — download VictronConnect
Search for VictronConnect in the App Store or Google Play and install it. It's free. This app is your window into the whole electrical system.
📶 Step 2 — connect to the SmartShunt
Open VictronConnect and it will scan for nearby Victron devices via Bluetooth. Tap the SmartShunt to connect. The default PIN is 000000 if prompted.
i You may see multiple Victron devices — SmartShunt, MPPT, Orion. Connect to the SmartShunt first for battery status.
📊 Step 3 — what you'll see
Once connected you'll see your battery state of charge as a percentage, voltage, and live amps flowing in or out. Positive amps = charging. Negative = discharging.
✓ Connect to the MPPT separately to see live solar input wattage. Both are worth bookmarking in the app.
